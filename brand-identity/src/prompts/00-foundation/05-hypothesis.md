# 05-hypothesis.md

**Purpose:** Generates three structurally distinct identity hypotheses from the foundational brief, pressure-tests each against the identity gate, and ranks them with a clear recommendation.
**When to Use:** After `02_foundational_brief.md` has been reviewed and confirmed.
**Dependencies:** `02_foundational_brief.md`, active lens file.
**Output:** `03_hypotheses_ranked.md`

---

## Pre-Hypothesis Rules

**Source discipline.**
Use only `02_foundational_brief.md` as input. Do not return to the original repository or the extraction. The brief is the authoritative source for this stage.

If prior knowledge about the company or its category influences a hypothesis, mark it explicitly: `[External — not from brief]`

**Structural distinction is mandatory.**
Three hypotheses means three different organising principles — not three tones, not three aesthetics, not three ways of saying the same thing.

Before completing the output, run the self-check below. If it fails, revise before delivering.

**Self-check — run before output:**
- Can each hypothesis be explained in one plain paragraph without reference to the others?
- Does each hypothesis make something impossible that the others allow?
- Would a designer working from Hypothesis A produce something that could not have come from Hypothesis B or C?

If any answer is no, the hypotheses are not distinct enough. Revise.

**Active tensions must be addressed.**
The brief carries forward unresolved tensions in Section 3. Each hypothesis must state explicitly how it positions itself relative to those tensions — whether it resolves them, holds them productively, or sidesteps them. Sidestepping must be named as such.

**No form at this stage.**
Do not describe logos, palettes, typefaces, or visual directions.
Define structural consequence. Form follows in the modules.

---

## Instruction

Read `src/outputs/00-foundation/02_foundational_brief.md` in full before generating any hypothesis.

Generate three structurally distinct identity hypotheses.

Apply the identity gate from the brief to each hypothesis and record the pressure test in full.

Rank all three and make a clear recommendation.

Output a single file: `src/outputs/00-foundation/03_hypotheses_ranked.md`

## Required Output Structure

---

## Hypothesis A

### Organising Principle

One sentence. The structural idea this hypothesis is built on.

### One-Paragraph Summary

Explain the hypothesis in plain language — what it is, what it does, why it is the right answer for this entity. No jargon. No hedging.

This is the lens final test applied in advance. If the argument does not stand in one paragraph, the hypothesis is not ready.

### Structural Logic

How does this organising principle govern identity, behaviour, and perception? Explain the internal logic — why does this principle produce the identity system it does?

Trace each claim to the brief.

### Tension Response

For each active tension identified in the brief (Section 3):
- How does this hypothesis position itself relative to that tension?
- Does it resolve it, hold it productively, or sidestep it?
- If it sidesteps: name this explicitly and state the consequence.

### What This Makes Inevitable

The consequences that follow necessarily from this principle. These are not preferences — they are structural outputs.

- Consequences for system behaviour
- Consequences for tone
- Consequences for form logic

### What This Makes Impossible

What this principle excludes. Be specific.

- What aesthetic directions are now off-limits
- What tonal registers cannot coexist with this logic
- What category signals must be rejected
- What the brand cannot do or claim under this hypothesis

If the list is short or vague, the principle is not strong enough.

### Long-Term Behaviour

- How does this mature over time?
- What happens under scale?
- What happens under market or cultural shift?
- Does it peak at launch or compound over time?

### Structural Risks

- Where is this hypothesis vulnerable?
- What does it depend on to succeed?
- What could destabilise it?
- Under what conditions would this need to be revisited?

### Identity Gate Pressure Test

Apply every gate question from `02_foundational_brief.md` Section 10.

For each question:
- **Pass / Conditional / Fail**
- One sentence justification

If any question results in Fail: state whether this is a fatal flaw or a resolvable condition.

## Human Handoff

This is the primary creative decision point in the process.

The machine has generated and ranked three directions. You now decide.

When reviewing `03_hypotheses_ranked.md`, consider:

1. Does the recommended hypothesis feel right for this entity — not just structurally correct, but directionally true?
2. Does any hypothesis surface something the brief didn't fully articulate?
3. Is there a direction not represented here that you want to introduce or explore?
4. Are the tensions handled in a way that feels honest to the subject?
5. Could any two hypotheses be combined without losing structural distinction? If yes, they weren't distinct — flag this.

You are not obligated to accept the recommendation. The ranking is a structural assessment, not a creative verdict.

Confirm your selected direction — or introduce an alternative — before proceeding to hypothesis lock.
