# Imagery System Generator

**Purpose:** Generates three structurally distinct imagery system directions derived from the active hypothesis. Produces conceptual exploration briefs — not art direction specifications or mood boards.
**When to Use:** After hypothesis lock.
**Dependencies:** `02_foundational_brief.md`, `04_active_hypothesis.md`
**Output:** `src/outputs/01-systems/imagery/imagery_concepts_vX.md`

---

## Pre-Module Rules

**Source discipline.**
Use only `02_foundational_brief.md` and `04_active_hypothesis.md` as inputs.
Do not import photographic styles, illustration tendencies, or visual references from previous projects or general design knowledge unless explicitly flagged: `[External — not from project files]`

**Tracing is required.**
Every concept must demonstrate how its imagery logic follows from the active hypothesis and brief.
Citation format: `[Hypothesis — section]` or `[Brief — Section X]`
If a concept cannot be traced, it is not ready.

**Structural distinction is mandatory.**
Three concepts means three different imagery logics — not three visual styles or photographic treatments. The distinction must be at the level of what imagery does in the system: how it constructs meaning, signals authority, and behaves structurally.

Self-check before output:
- Does each concept follow from the hypothesis in a different way?
- Does each concept make imagery behave differently as a structural element?
- Would a designer working from Concept 1 make fundamentally different art direction decisions than from Concept 2 or 3?

If any answer is no, revise before delivering.

**No mood board language.**
Do not describe imagery through atmosphere, feeling, or aesthetic reference.
Define what imagery does, how it functions in the system, and what it must communicate structurally.

---

## Instruction

Read `src/outputs/00-foundation/04_active_hypothesis.md` and `src/outputs/00-foundation/02_foundational_brief.md` in full before generating any concept.

Generate three structurally distinct imagery system directions.

Apply the identity gate from the brief to each concept and record the pressure test in full.

Rank all three and make a clear recommendation.

Output a single file: `src/outputs/01-systems/imagery/imagery_concepts_vX.md`

## Required Output Structure

---

## Concept 1 — [Short Structural Name]

### Grounding Statement

In one sentence: how does this concept follow from the active hypothesis?

Cite: `[Hypothesis — Organising Principle]` and `[Brief — Section X]`

If this cannot be written clearly, the concept is not grounded. Do not proceed with it.

---

### Structural Imagery Logic

- What role does imagery play in this system — how does it construct meaning rather than decorate?
- How does this imagery approach express the organising principle?
- How does it signal or reframe category position?

---

### Subject & Content Logic

- What is shown — and the structural reason for that choice
- What is never shown — and why
- Human presence: present, absent, peripheral, central — and the structural reason
- Product / service depiction: how and when
- Abstract vs concrete — where the system sits and why

---

### Formal Behaviour

- Framing and composition logic (what the organising principle demands spatially)
- Scale and cropping strategy
- Relationship between imagery and other system elements (type, space, colour)
- How imagery interacts with the grid/layout system

---

### Treatment Logic

- Level of post-production: minimal, editorial, stylised — and the structural reason
- Colour treatment within imagery (natural, reduced, monochrome, etc.) and its relationship to the colour system
- What treatment would break the system

---

### Cross-Context Behaviour

How the imagery system behaves across different surfaces:
- Product UI / in-app
- Marketing / web
- Documentation / reports
- Print / physical (if relevant)
- Social / editorial

For each: what imagery must accomplish and where it is most stressed.

---

### What This Makes Impossible

- Imagery types that are structurally excluded
- Photographic or illustrative territories that would mis-signal
- Treatments that cannot coexist with this logic

If the list is vague, the concept is not strong enough.

---

### Category & Mis-Signal Risk

- What category or competitor territory this could accidentally signal
- Where it could drift under production pressure (stock photography, trend-led art direction)
- How the risk is mitigated structurally

---

### Identity Gate Pressure Test

Apply every gate question from `02_foundational_brief.md` Section 10.

For each question:
- **Pass / Conditional / Fail**
- One sentence justification

If any question results in Fail: state whether this is a fatal flaw or a resolvable condition.

---

### Exploration Brief

What the designer / art director should begin from.

- The first art direction decision to make
- The primary tension to test (e.g. abstract vs human, editorial vs documentary)
- What to commission or source first as a test
- What to avoid immediately — the specific traps this concept is most vulnerable to

## Human Handoff

When reviewing `imagery_concepts_vX.md`, consider:

1. Does the recommended concept's imagery logic feel right for how this brand needs to represent itself?
2. Does the subject and content logic reflect the actual reality of this entity — what it does, who it serves?
3. Is there an imagery approach not represented here that you want to explore?
4. Are the exploration briefs specific enough to begin art direction work from?
5. Does any concept risk drifting into stock photography territory or category-generic imagery?

You may select the recommendation, choose a different concept, introduce a new direction, or request a revised set. If introducing a new direction, define its grounding statement before beginning work.

Confirm your selected direction before proceeding.
