# Layout System Generator

**Purpose:** Generates three structurally distinct layout system directions derived from the active hypothesis. Produces conceptual exploration briefs — not grid specifications or finished compositions.
**When to Use:** After hypothesis lock.
**Dependencies:** `02_foundational_brief.md`, `04_active_hypothesis.md`
**Output:** `layout_concepts_vX.md`

---

## Pre-Module Rules

**Source discipline.**
Use only `02_foundational_brief.md` and `04_active_hypothesis.md` as inputs.
Do not import layout tendencies, grid preferences, or compositional instincts from previous projects or general design knowledge unless explicitly flagged: `[External — not from project files]`

**Tracing is required.**
Every concept must demonstrate how its spatial logic follows from the active hypothesis and brief.
Citation format: `[Hypothesis — section]` or `[Brief — Section X]`
If a concept cannot be traced, it is not ready.

**Structural distinction is mandatory.**
Three concepts means three different spatial logics — not three visual styles. The distinction must be at the level of how space enforces meaning, how hierarchy is structured, and how the layout reflects the organising principle.

Self-check before output:
- Does each concept follow from the hypothesis in a different way?
- Does each concept make fundamentally different spatial decisions?
- Would a designer working from Concept 1 produce a system that could not have come from Concept 2 or 3?

If any answer is no, revise before delivering.

**Describe structural behaviour, not mood.**
Do not use words like "airy", "bold", "clean" without structural justification.
Every spatial decision must trace to the organising principle.

---

## Instruction

Read `src/outputs/00-foundation/04_active_hypothesis.md` and `src/outputs/00-foundation/02_foundational_brief.md` in full before generating any concept.

Generate three structurally distinct layout system directions.

Apply the identity gate from the brief to each concept and record the pressure test in full.

Rank all three and make a clear recommendation.

Output a single file: `src/outputs/01-systems/layout_concepts_vX.md`

## Required Output Structure

---

## Concept 1 — [Short Structural Name]

### Grounding Statement

In one sentence: how does this concept follow from the active hypothesis?

Cite: `[Hypothesis — Organising Principle]` and `[Brief — Section X]`

If this cannot be written clearly, the concept is not grounded. Do not proceed with it.

---

### Structural Spatial Logic

- What spatial principle governs this system?
- How does it express the organising principle through the use of space?
- Core grid philosophy: rigid, modular, adaptive, asymmetric, freeform — and the structural reason for this choice

---

### Hierarchy Behaviour

- How primary vs secondary information is structured spatially
- How emphasis is created — and what spatial devices are forbidden
- How attention flows through the system
- What the layout refuses to do

---

### Density & Rhythm Strategy

- Sparse vs dense — and what the organising principle demands
- Predictability vs disruption — where each is permitted
- Repetition logic and what it communicates
- White space philosophy: structural or decorative?

---

### Containment Model

- Framed vs open — and the structural reason
- Bounded modules vs fluid composition
- How the containment approach reflects the organising principle
- What containment decisions signal about authority and posture

---

### Cross-Context Behaviour

How the system behaves across different surfaces:
- Product UI
- Marketing / web
- Long-form content / documentation
- Presentations (if relevant)

For each: what the layout system must accomplish and where it is most stressed.

---

### What This Makes Impossible

- Spatial approaches that are structurally excluded
- Compositional styles that would mis-signal
- Layout behaviours that cannot coexist with this logic

If the list is vague, the concept is not strong enough.

---

### Category & Mis-Signal Risk

- What category this could accidentally signal through spatial choices
- Where it could drift under production pressure
- How the risk is mitigated structurally

---

### Identity Gate Pressure Test

Apply every gate question from `02_foundational_brief.md` Section 10.

For each question:
- **Pass / Conditional / Fail**
- One sentence justification

If any question results in Fail: state whether this is a fatal flaw or a resolvable condition.

---

### Exploration Brief

What the designer should begin from.

- The spatial decision to define first (grid, hierarchy, containment)
- The primary tension to test (e.g. structure vs flexibility, density vs openness)
- What to prototype across contexts before committing
- What to avoid immediately — the specific traps this concept is most vulnerable to

## Human Handoff

When reviewing `layout_concepts_vX.md`, consider:

1. Does the recommended concept's spatial logic feel right for how this brand needs to communicate?
2. Does the hierarchy behaviour reflect the actual information demands of this entity?
3. Is there a spatial approach not represented here that you want to explore?
4. Are the exploration briefs specific enough to begin grid and composition work from?
5. Does any concept risk signalling the wrong category through its spatial choices?

You may select the recommendation, choose a different concept, introduce a new direction, or request a revised set. If introducing a new direction, define its grounding statement before beginning work.

Confirm your selected direction before proceeding.
