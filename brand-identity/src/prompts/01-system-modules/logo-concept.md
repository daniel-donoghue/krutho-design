# Logo Concept Generator

**Purpose:** Generates three structurally distinct logo directions derived from the active hypothesis. Produces conceptual exploration briefs to initiate design work — not finished specifications.
**When to Use:** After hypothesis lock.
**Dependencies:** `02_foundational_brief.md`, `04_active_hypothesis.md`
**Output:** `src/outputs/01-systems/logo/logo_concepts_vX.md`

---

## Pre-Module Rules

**Source discipline.**
Use only `02_foundational_brief.md` and `04_active_hypothesis.md` as inputs.
Do not return to the original repository or extraction.
Do not import logo styles, geometric tropes, metaphors, or aesthetic instincts from previous projects or general design knowledge unless explicitly flagged: `[External — not from project files]`

**Tracing is required.**
Every concept must demonstrate how its structural logic follows from the active hypothesis and brief. This is not optional — it is the test of whether the concept is grounded.
Citation format: `[Hypothesis — section]` or `[Brief — Section X]`
If a concept cannot be traced, it is not ready.

**Structural distinction is mandatory.**
Three concepts means three different structural logics — not three aesthetic treatments of the same idea.

Before completing the output, run this self-check:
- Does each concept follow from the hypothesis in a different way?
- Does each concept make something impossible that the others allow?
- Would a designer working from Concept 1 produce something irreconcilably different from Concept 2 or 3?

If any answer is no, revise before delivering.

**No surface form at this stage.**
Do not describe finished logos, specific typefaces, or colour treatments.
Define mark logic, construction principles, and structural behaviour.
Form follows from the exploration brief — that is the designer's work.

---

## Instruction

Read `src/outputs/00-foundation/04_active_hypothesis.md` and `src/outputs/00-foundation/02_foundational_brief.md` in full before generating any concept.

Generate three structurally distinct logo directions.

Apply the identity gate from the brief to each concept and record the pressure test in full.

Rank all three and make a clear recommendation.

Output a single file: `src/outputs/01-systems/logo/logo_concepts_vX.md`

## Required Output Structure

---

## Concept 1 — [Short Structural Name]

### Grounding Statement

In one sentence: how does this concept follow from the active hypothesis?

Cite: `[Hypothesis — Organising Principle]` and `[Brief — Section X]`

If this cannot be written clearly, the concept is not grounded. Do not proceed with it.

---

### Structural Logic

- What structural idea governs this mark?
- How does it express the organising principle — not illustrate it, express it?
- How does it signal category position or reframe it?

---

### Construction Logic

- Core geometry or construction approach (contained, open, modular, singular, layered, etc.)
- Relationship between symbol and wordmark (if both are present)
- Degree of abstraction vs literal reference — and the structural reason for that degree
- Static vs dynamic logic: does this scale into system behaviours beyond the mark itself?

---

### What This Makes Inevitable

The consequences that follow necessarily from this structural logic:
- Form tendencies (what kinds of marks naturally emerge from this)
- System implications (how this logic extends into other elements)
- Behavioural posture (how this mark "acts" in context)

---

### What This Makes Impossible

What this concept structurally excludes. Be specific enough to use as a real filter.
- Mark types that are now off-limits
- Metaphors or iconographic territory that cannot coexist
- Aesthetic directions that are closed

If the list is short or vague, the concept is not strong enough.

---

### Scalability & Durability

- Behaviour at small scale
- Behaviour in monochrome
- Behaviour without colour support
- Behaviour in motion contexts (if relevant)
- Would this survive significant cultural or market shift? Why?

---

### Category & Mis-Signal Risk

- What category could this accidentally signal?
- What existing brand territory does it risk resembling?
- How is that risk mitigated structurally (not stylistically)?

---

### Identity Gate Pressure Test

Apply every gate question from `02_foundational_brief.md` Section 10.

For each question:
- **Pass / Conditional / Fail**
- One sentence justification

If any question results in Fail: state whether this is a fatal flaw or a resolvable condition.

---

### Exploration Brief

What the designer should begin from. This is the handoff into making.

- The structural territory to explore first
- The primary tension to test in sketching
- What to prototype in parallel (if any)
- What to avoid immediately — the specific traps this concept is vulnerable to

## Human Handoff

The machine has generated and ranked three logo directions. You now decide what to explore.

When reviewing `logo_concepts_vX.md`, consider:

1. Does the recommended concept feel directionally right for this entity — not just structurally correct?
2. Does any concept surface a mark logic you want to explore that isn't fully developed here?
3. Is there a structural territory not represented that you want to introduce?
4. Are the exploration briefs specific enough to start sketching from?
5. Could any two concepts be merged without losing structural distinction? If yes, flag it — they weren't distinct.

You are not obligated to follow the recommendation. You may:
- Select the recommended concept and begin exploration
- Select a different concept and begin exploration
- Introduce a new direction not generated here — if so, define its grounding statement before beginning work
- Ask for a revised set if none of the concepts feel grounded in the hypothesis

Confirm your selected direction before beginning design exploration.
