## Logo — Direction selected

**Decision:** Concept 3 — The Wordmark. The wordmark is the entire identity; letterform precision is the structural argument. No symbol.
**Alternatives:** Concept 1 (The Notation) ranked second — equal gate performance, notation mark as anchor if wordmark alone proves insufficient. Concept 2 (The Container) ranked third — most execution-dependent, four conditional gate results.
**Notes:** "The most honest solution. Structurally pure — more in line with the behaviour of X.509, HTTP, TLS. No symbol. A wordmark feels serious. Typographic architecture that can withstand use across GitHub, npm listings, CLI outputs, slides, whitepapers."

---

## Logo — Settled

**Date:** 2026-03-02
**Decision:** Concept 3 — The Wordmark. Final wordmark confirmed. Monolinear construction, 100-unit stroke weight, no symbol. Key structural properties fixed: mathematically constructed with optical adjustments limited to `u` and `h` tapered arches; `r` as code-adjacent letterform setting technical register; `o` as rounded rectangle. No further direction exploration in scope.
**Alternatives:** Concept 1 (The Notation) — not pursued; Concept 2 (The Container) — not pursued. No exploration paths abandoned mid-process; the wordmark was developed directly from the selected concept.
**Notes:** "The design was strong enough visually to appear as more than a font. The 'correctness' of the design gives it a system feel that's built on process, but there's a human readability to it without becoming a font. The slight awkwardness created by being mathematically correct is what makes it feel human."
