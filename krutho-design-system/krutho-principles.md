# Krutho Design Principles

Last edit 23rd April 2026

---

The Krutho design system operates under eight principles. Each principle is a value the work holds. Together they form the frame within which design decisions are made and judged.

---

## Purposeful

Every design decision serves a job. The job is named, and the decision is judged against it. Purpose is the first criterion.

## Honest

Properties are demonstrated, not performed. Credibility is earned by evidence, not signalled by style. Honesty is the disposition that makes the system trustable.

## Understandable

The work communicates what it is. Anyone reading it, designer, developer, stakeholder, machine, can know what it is saying. Clarity is the condition of use.

## Coherent

The parts agree with each other. Decisions made at one level hold at every other level. The system reads as one thing because it is one thing.

## Restrained

Only what earns its place is present. Restraint is a craft discipline. What is excluded is decided with the same care as what is included.

## Considered

The work is resolved. Every decision is followed through to detail. Craft is what separates work that holds up from work that does not.

## Durable

The work holds across surfaces, contexts, and time. Decisions made today remain legible and defensible later. Durability is earned by being made well.

## Aesthetic

Form supports the work. Beauty is the visible result of a thing being well made. Aesthetic judgment is welcome and held to the same standard as every other decision.
